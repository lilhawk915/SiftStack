## What changed since v2

Single commit closes the 73% probate recall ceiling identified in `backtest_2026-06-26_v2.md`. The portal's year-wide listing was hard-capped at ~344 cases of ~2300 actual filings (verified empirically against 2025); quiet/closed cases were silently excluded. The fix:

* Adds `_gap_fill_date_window` to `MontgomeryProbateScraper`. After the main case-detail capture loop, the scraper identifies the case# range `[min, max]` of cases whose `date_filed` is inside the window, then probes every missing case# in that range via the portal's single-case search (`caseyear` + `casenbr`). Single-case search returns reliable results — it's only the year-wide listing that's sparse.
* Per-case# search isn't affected by the ~344 cap. Verified: `casenbr=00895` returns EST00895 cleanly even though the year-wide query for 2026 omits it.

Tracerfy + Trestle stayed off (`ONBASE_ENABLED=1` only). `case_number` propagation (b2039cb) and `--max-cases` flag (75da6d5) from yesterday are still in place.

## What's still missing — the FC short-form# cases

The 2 remaining FC recall misses (`2026 CV 0440` and `2026 CV 0517`) both use a non-standard short-form case-number format (4 digits instead of the typical 5). The DM sheet writes them as `2026 CV 0517`; SiftStack normalized to `2026CV517`. These are likely legacy / re-opened cases — they appear in the DM sheet but not in SiftStack's pro.mcohio.org foreclosure scrape at all. Investigation would need a fresh look at the foreclosure portal's listing logic (similar to what just happened for probate). Out of scope for today.
