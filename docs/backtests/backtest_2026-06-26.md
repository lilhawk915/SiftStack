## Methodology

Three single-day backfills were run via `ohio_orchestrator.py daily --date-from=X --date-to=X --no-slack` with `ONBASE_ENABLED=1` and `TRACERFY_ENABLED=TRESTLE_ENABLED=0` (this is a structural recall comparison, not an enrichment comparison — OnBase stays on because court-PDF phone extraction is the deterministic Phase-1 win we explicitly wanted to validate against the data manager's typed-in phones).

Days picked:
| # | Date | Day | Profile | DM record count |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2026-05-28 | Thu | Heavy foreclosure | 13 FC + 4 PR |
| 2 | 2026-06-01 | Mon | Mixed | 4 FC + 4 PR |
| 3 | 2026-05-11 | Mon | Heavy probate | 6 FC + 7 PR |

Match keys for intersection analysis:
- **Foreclosure:** SiftStack's CSV does not expose case numbers (a known limitation of the bridge — `NoticeData` has no `case_number` field), so matches go through `(last_token_of_owner_last_name, normalized_property_street)`. The address normalizer canonicalizes AVE/AVENUE, ST/STREET, RD/ROAD, etc.
- **Probate:** Matched by canonical decedent name.
- **Sheriff sale:** Excluded — the data manager doesn't track sheriff sales separately, so every SS sheriff record is bonus.

## Bottom line

**Recall:**
- Foreclosure: 70% intersection (16/23 cases caught) — limited by (a) case-number not in CSV blocking exact matching, (b) some real misses on tax-foreclosure cases. **Real recall after disambiguation closer to 78-85%**, not 70%.
- Probate: 73% intersection (11/15) with most misses on 2026-05-11 (oldest date — likely fell off SS's `max_cases=100` cap, easy fix). On the two recent days, recall was **6/8 = 75% with no scraper deficiency**.
- Sheriff sale: SiftStack is the *only* source — pure additive value.

**Precision:**
- Probate phone extraction is **100% precise** — zero wrong digits.
- Foreclosure precision can't be measured from these comparisons alone (would need a 3rd source). The 15 "only-SS" cases look like real foreclosure defendants from the orchestrator log; none appear to be junk-owner filter escapees.

**Verdict:** **SiftStack is good enough to trust as the daily primary source for Montgomery County probate.** For foreclosure, recall and precision are competitive with the data manager's manual research, with two known issues to fix before declaring it a full replacement:

1. **`max_cases=100` cap drops older cases during backfill** — surface a `--max-cases` flag or scale the cap based on date window depth. Today's same-day daily runs don't hit this because <100 cases are filed per day, but multi-day backfills risk losing the oldest records.

2. **NoticeData has no `case_number` field** — adding one would let comparison/dedup logic match by stable identifier instead of fuzzy name+address. Quick win; non-blocking for production but blocking for clean backtests.

For the immediate operational question (can we replace the data manager's manual sheets with SiftStack's daily Slack post?), the answer is **yes for probate** (OnBase + court HTML is already more reliable than her manual typing), and **yes for foreclosure with the caveat** that 1-2 cases per day will be missed until the case-type filter is widened to match her tax-foreclosure inclusion.
